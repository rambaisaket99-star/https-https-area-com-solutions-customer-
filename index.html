We study the empirical scaling laws for language model performance on cross-entropy loss and examine how these laws extend when context windows are dramatically increased beyond training distribution. We find new scaling behaviors emerge that challenge existing predictions.

We present a comprehensive analysis of how specific attention heads in transformer models perform identifiable, human-interpretable computations. Using activation patching and causal tracing, we identify heads responsible for induction, copying, and factual recall operations.

Our Values

What we believe about AI

01
Safety First
We believe safe and helpful AI are more complementary than they are at odds. Our Constitutional AI approach ensures our models behave consistently with human values even in edge cases.
02
Radical Honesty
We don't just want AI that says what users want to hear. Area models are trained to be genuinely helpful, which sometimes means respectfully disagreeing or acknowledging uncertainty.
03
Long-term Thinking
We're building for the decades ahead, not the next quarter. That means thoughtful decisions about capability development, deployment practices, and our broader impact on society.
04
Open Science
We publish our research, share our findings with the broader community, and believe that open collaboration is the best way to ensure AI development benefits everyone.
05
Diverse Perspectives
The most important decisions about AI should reflect the diversity of people affected by it. We actively work to build a team and consider stakeholders from all backgrounds.
06
Iterative Improvement
We release thoughtfully, learn from real-world use, and continuously improve. We'd rather ship something good and iterate than wait indefinitely for something perfect.
