Our Mission
"Our mission is to develop AI that is safe, beneficial, and comprehensible — not just capable."

We started Area because we believed the AI safety and AI capability communities were treating those goals as trade-offs when they're actually complements. The safest AI systems are also the most reliable, the most honest, and ultimately the most useful.

We're building toward a world where highly capable AI systems are deployed responsibly — where the benefits are broadly shared, where risks are carefully managed, and where the technology genuinely augments human potential rather than replacing human judgment wholesale.

That's a hard problem. We don't have all the answers. But we think the best approach is to be transparent about what we know and don't know, to publish our research, to engage with critics honestly, and to build the infrastructure for AI development that is accountable to the people it serves.

Values

What we believe about AI

01
Safety and helpfulness are complements
We reject the framing that safety and capability are at odds. Our Constitutional AI approach proves that models can be trained to be genuinely helpful precisely because they have principled limits — not in spite of them. Safe models are also trustworthy models, and trustworthy models get used more.
02
Radical honesty — even when it's uncomfortable
We don't want AI that tells people what they want to hear. Area models are trained to be genuinely honest, which sometimes means respectfully disagreeing, acknowledging uncertainty, or flagging when a request might lead to harm. Sycophantic AI is dangerous AI.
03
Long-term thinking over short-term wins
We're building for the decades ahead, not just the next quarter. That means making thoughtful decisions about which capabilities to develop and when, how we deploy systems, and how we engage with the broader ecosystem — even when slower decisions cost us in the short term.
04
Open science and collaborative progress
We publish our research, engage openly with critics, and believe that the AI safety community as a whole benefits when knowledge is shared widely. Competitive advantage built on secrecy makes everyone less safe. We'd rather win on execution than information asymmetry.
05
Diverse perspectives improve outcomes
The most consequential decisions about AI should reflect the diversity of people affected by it. We actively recruit across backgrounds, engage external experts in ethics, policy, and safety, and hold ourselves accountable to communities beyond the tech industry.
06
Iterate openly, improve continuously
We'd rather release good work and improve openly than wait indefinitely for perfection. We publish model cards, share evaluation results — including failures — and update our practices based on what we learn in real-world deployment. Honest imperfection beats opaque polish.
